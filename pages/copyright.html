Copyright and left

In this section you will find some Copyright notices, my and some other people's take on Copyright and Copyleft, as well as some sources for regarding Copyleft explainations and license examples.

Copyleft licensing

Copyright is useful to protect your intellectual property. In itself that is not a bad thing. You should decide how the works you create can or can't be used. However, if you are not sure how you should license your next project, consider publishing it under a Copyleft license, such as the GNU General Public License v3.0 for software, or the Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International Public License for other works. With such license you enable everybody to freely build their works based on yours, as long as they also allow other people to create new works under the same copyleft license. That way your work is free to use - free as in freedom.

In case you would like to get a brief introduction to these licenses you can check out the videos What are the GPL and LGPL and how do they differ? from Todd Harris and Wanna Work Together? from Creative Commons. For those familiar with open source software, it may also be interesting to have a look at the article Why Open Source misses the point of Free Software by Richard Stallman.
